thoughtware

Tools for thought (or thoughtware) are tools which expand the range of what and how people can think. Building tools for thought (or engaging in thoughtware engineering) is the process of creating and recursively making use of such tools. There are countless approaches to this, but I'm focusing on creating composable building blocks which amplify basic cognitive functions. In this, I'm trying to lay the groundwork for tomorrow's cognitive infrastructure. And yes, I made up half the terms on this page.

Past projects:
  • dual: amplifying knowledge work through user-defined assistants.
  • autocards: accelerating learning through machine-generated flashcards.
  • semantica: extending conceptual thinking through semantic embeddings.
  • memnav: expanding propositional memory through text mining.
  • k-probes: promoting critical thinking through prompt generation.
  • eeg2fmri: enhancing neuroimaging through cross-modal synthesis.
